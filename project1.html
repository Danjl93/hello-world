Johannesburg RSA, Down a goal with under three minutes to go, the United States's tied the final of the 2017 Ladies Hockey World League Semi-Final on a Taylor West penalty stroke. They would go on to win the the shootout when the youngest player on the field, 17-year-old Erin Matson, slotted home the decider to end a dramatic shootout 3-2. Heavily out possessed by the smooth passing Germans, who belted the US defense with shots, it was veteran keeper Jackie Briggs who put in a terrific performance which also netted her the tournament Best Goalkeeper honors. Briggs produced her best in the shootout when she set up her team for the win with this gem of a save. In addition earning their first ever title in the World Hockey League, the USA also clinched a spot in next years Women's Hockey World Cup in London.
